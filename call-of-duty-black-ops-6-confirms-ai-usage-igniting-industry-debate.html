Call of Duty: Black Ops 6 Confirms AI Usage, Igniting Industry Debate

By Felix
March 1, 2025

For years, the gaming industry has been abuzz with speculation about the integration of Artificial Intelligence (AI) in game development. From enhancing non-player character (NPC) behaviors to streamlining asset creation, the potential applications of AI are vast and transformative. Now, one of the biggest franchises in gaming history, Call of Duty, has officially stepped into the AI arena, confirming its use of AI in the upcoming Black Ops 6.

The revelation, disclosed on the game's Steam page, has sent ripples through the gaming community and development circles alike. While some see it as a natural evolution, a way to boost efficiency and innovation, others express concerns about the implications for artistic integrity, job security for human developers, and the overall quality of game experiences.

This article delves into the confirmation of AI usage in Call of Duty: Black Ops 6, exploring the context, the reactions, and the broader implications of this move for the future of game development.

The Steam Disclosure: A Quiet Confirmation

The confirmation of AI involvement in Black Ops 6 wasn't announced with fanfare or a press release. Instead, it was subtly added to the game's Steam page under an unassuming section titled 'AI Generated Content Disclosure'. The description is concise, stating: "Our team is using AI-powered creation tools to create some of the game's features." This brief statement, while lacking specifics, officially acknowledges what many had suspected â€“ AI is part of the Black Ops 6 development process.

This quiet disclosure contrasts sharply with the magnitude of the news. Call of Duty is a colossal franchise, consistently breaking sales records and drawing in millions of players worldwide. For such a flagship title to openly admit to using AI is a significant moment, signaling a potential shift in how major game studios approach development.

The timing of the disclosure is also noteworthy. While the Steam page listing might have been recently noticed by players, data from SteamDB, a site that tracks changes on Steam, reveals that the 'AI Generated Content Disclosure' was registered a month prior, on January 27th. This suggests that the decision to disclose was not a last-minute reaction, but a planned inclusion, possibly coinciding with Steam's new policy requiring developers to disclose any use of AI content. This new policy, announced in January 2024, aims to ensure transparency and prevent the distribution of "illegal or infringing content" on the platform, making AI disclosures visible on game store pages.

The fact that players only recently noticed the disclosure may be attributed to the recent introduction of SteamDB's new filter, designed to identify games utilizing AI. This highlights a growing awareness and interest among players in understanding the role of AI in the games they play.

Months of Speculation and the Zombie Santa

While the Steam disclosure might seem sudden, eagle-eyed players have been pointing to potential signs of AI-generated content in Call of Duty: Black Ops 6 for months. The most prominent example emerged with the Season 1 Reloaded update. As reported by 80 Level in December, a loading screen for this update featured an image of a zombie Santa Claus, dubbed "Necroclaus," that exhibited telltale signs of AI generation.

These signs included:

  • Anomalous Hands: The zombie Santa was depicted with an unusual left hand pose and, most strikingly, six fingers on his right hand. Hands, particularly fingers, have become a notorious stumbling block for many AI image generators, often resulting in extra or misplaced digits.
  • Strange Ribbons: The ribbons adorning the zombie Santa also appeared unnatural and strangely rendered, lacking the coherent flow and detail expected from human-created artwork.

These visual anomalies didn't go unnoticed by the Call of Duty community. Players took to online forums and social media to discuss the possibility of AI involvement, with many expressing concern and disappointment. The "Necroclaus" loading screen became a focal point of debate, with some labeling it "AI slop" and criticizing the perceived decline in visual quality.

IGN also highlighted another image from the Season 1 Reloaded update, featuring a gloved hand used to explain a new Zombies community event. This image also appeared to depict six fingers, further fueling speculation about AI-generated assets within the game.

These instances, predating the official Steam disclosure, suggest that Activision's use of AI in Black Ops 6 was not a recent development but rather an ongoing practice. The Steam disclosure serves as a formal acknowledgment of a trend that players had already begun to identify and scrutinize.

Activision's Previous AI Ventures and the Layoff Context

This isn't Activision's first foray into the world of AI. As Wired reported in July 2024, Activision had already sold a cosmetic item in Call of Duty: Modern Warfare 3 that was allegedly "AI-generated." This cosmetic was linked to the Yokai's Wrath bundle, released in December 2023, and was sold for 1,500 COD Points, a premium virtual currency worth approximately $15. Notably, the store listing for this bundle did not disclose the use of AI in its creation.

The Wired report further alleged that Activision had greenlit the use of AI tools for concept art and marketing materials as early as mid-2023, with implementation expanding by the end of that year. This timeline aligns with the timeline of Microsoft's acquisition of Activision Blizzard in October 2024 for $69 billion. Shortly after this acquisition, Microsoft implemented significant layoffs, cutting 1,900 jobs from its gaming division.

Adding to the controversy, the Wired report cited an anonymous Activision artist who claimed that "a lot of 2D artists were laid off" and that remaining concept artists were "then forced to use AI to aid in their work." The report also stated that Activision employees were allegedly "made" to sign up for AI training, indicating a company-wide push towards AI integration.

This context of layoffs and alleged pressure to adopt AI tools adds another layer of complexity to the Black Ops 6 AI disclosure. It raises questions about whether AI is being used to enhance creativity and efficiency, or if it's being implemented as a cost-cutting measure, potentially at the expense of human jobs and artistic quality.

Player and Developer Reactions: A Mix of Concern and Skepticism

The confirmation of AI usage in Black Ops 6 has been met with a mixed bag of reactions from players and industry professionals. While some acknowledge the potential benefits of AI in game development, the prevailing sentiment appears to be one of concern and skepticism.

Player Concerns:

  • Quality Concerns: A primary concern among players is the potential impact on game quality. The "Necroclaus" loading screen incident serves as a stark example of the flaws that can arise from AI-generated content, particularly in visual assets. Players worry that relying on AI could lead to a decline in artistic quality, resulting in generic, uninspired, or visually inconsistent game worlds. Online discussions have seen AI-generated content derisively labeled as "AI slop," reflecting a lack of confidence in its ability to match human artistry.
  • Authenticity and Artistic Integrity: Many gamers value the human element in game development. They appreciate the craftsmanship, creativity, and personal touch that human artists bring to game worlds and characters. There's a fear that over-reliance on AI will erode this authenticity, leading to game experiences that feel less unique and more mass-produced. Players express a desire for "handcrafted experiences" and worry that AI-generated assets will result in "repetitive or soulless designs."
  • Ethical Concerns and Job Displacement: Beyond quality, ethical considerations are also at play. Many players are concerned about the potential for AI to displace human artists and developers within the gaming industry. In a climate of industry-wide layoffs, the increased use of AI is seen by some as a threat to creative jobs. The fact that Activision, a highly profitable company, is adopting AI raises questions about whether this is driven by necessity or a desire to maximize profits at the expense of human talent.
  • Lack of Transparency: The initial lack of transparency surrounding AI usage in Black Ops 6, and the subtle nature of the Steam disclosure, have also fueled player concerns. The fact that players had to identify potential AI-generated content themselves before an official acknowledgment was made has eroded trust for some. Players are now calling for greater transparency from developers regarding their use of AI, particularly in games they are paying for.

Developer Concerns:

The concerns aren't limited to players; game developers themselves are also grappling with the implications of AI in their field. A recent GDC (Game Developers Conference) survey sheds light on the anxieties within the development community.

  • Widespread AI Usage and Worry: The GDC survey revealed that nearly 50% of game developers reported that AI-powered tools were already being used in their workplaces. A significant majority, 84%, expressed worry about the impact of AI on their productivity. This suggests a widespread adoption of AI tools within the industry, coupled with considerable unease among developers about its effects.
  • Productivity Concerns: The survey highlights a paradoxical concern: while AI is often touted as a tool to boost productivity, a large majority of developers are worried about its negative impact on their productivity. This could stem from concerns about the quality of AI-generated content, the need for extensive manual correction and refinement, or the potential for AI to disrupt established workflows and creative processes.
  • Tool Adoption: The survey also revealed the specific AI tools being used by developers. 31% of respondents reported using tools like ChatGPT, DALL-E, GitHub Copilot, and Adobe Generative Fill in their work. Another 18% indicated that while they personally didn't use these tools, their colleagues did. This demonstrates a significant level of adoption of readily available generative AI tools within game development studios.

SAG-AFTRA and Voice Actor Concerns:

The use of AI in Call of Duty: Black Ops 6 has also intersected with ongoing labor disputes and concerns about AI's impact on voice actors. SAG-AFTRA, the actors' union, has been actively advocating for ethical safeguards and creative integrity in AI implementation, particularly concerning the use of voice samples.

Reports have indicated that voice actors for Black Ops 6 have quit over AI disputes. The union is pushing for clear boundaries on how voice samples can be manipulated or replicated using AI, seeking to protect the rights and livelihoods of voice actors in the face of advancing AI technology. The controversy surrounding the "Necroclaus" loading screen was further exacerbated by the context of Zombies mode voice actors being potentially replaced amidst the SAG-AFTRA strike, highlighting the real-world implications of AI adoption for creative professionals.

Potential Benefits and the Future of AI in Game Development

Despite the widespread concerns, it's important to acknowledge that AI also offers potential benefits to game development. Many developers see AI as a tool to enhance, not replace, human creativity.

Potential Advantages of AI in Game Development:

  • Streamlined Asset Creation: Generative AI tools can significantly speed up the creation of certain game assets, such as textures, concept art, environmental details, and background elements. This can free up human artists to focus on more complex and creatively demanding tasks.
  • Rapid Prototyping and Iteration: AI can assist in rapid prototyping, allowing developers to quickly experiment with different ideas and iterate on designs. This can accelerate the development process and lead to more innovative gameplay mechanics and visual styles.
  • Enhanced NPC Behavior: AI can be used to create more dynamic and believable NPC behaviors, making game worlds feel more alive and responsive. AI-powered NPCs could react more realistically to player actions, adapt to changing game environments, and offer more engaging interactions.
  • Procedural Content Generation: AI algorithms can generate vast amounts of content procedurally, creating diverse and expansive game worlds with less manual effort. This can be particularly useful for open-world games or games with procedurally generated levels.
  • Accessibility and Inclusivity: AI could potentially be used to enhance game accessibility, for example, by generating real-time captions, translating in-game text, or adapting game difficulty based on player skill level.

The Path Forward: Collaboration and Ethical Considerations

The integration of AI in game development is not a question of if, but how. The Call of Duty: Black Ops 6 disclosure highlights the need for a balanced and ethical approach to AI adoption. The future of AI in game development likely lies in collaboration between human creativity and AI tools, rather than outright replacement.

Key Considerations for the Future:

  • Transparency and Disclosure: Developers need to be more transparent with players about their use of AI. Clear disclosures, like the one on the Black Ops 6 Steam page, are a step in the right direction, but more detailed information about how AI is being used would be beneficial.
  • Focus on Enhancement, Not Replacement: The focus should be on using AI to augment and enhance human creativity, not to replace human artists and developers. AI should be seen as a tool to assist human creators, freeing them up to focus on higher-level creative tasks and artistic direction.
  • Maintaining Artistic Integrity: Developers need to prioritize artistic integrity and quality control when using AI. AI-generated content should be carefully reviewed and refined by human artists to ensure it meets the desired aesthetic standards and fits cohesively within the game world.
  • Ethical Guidelines and Labor Protections: The industry needs to develop ethical guidelines for AI usage, addressing concerns about job displacement, fair compensation for artists, and the responsible use of AI-generated content. Unions like SAG-AFTRA are playing a crucial role in advocating for these protections.
  • Player Dialogue and Feedback: Open dialogue with players is essential. Developers should listen to player feedback regarding AI usage and be responsive to concerns about quality, authenticity, and ethical implications.

Conclusion: Navigating the AI Frontier in Gaming

The confirmation of AI usage in Call of Duty: Black Ops 6 marks a significant moment in the gaming industry. It's a clear signal that AI is no longer a futuristic concept but a present reality in game development. While concerns and skepticism are valid and need to be addressed, the potential benefits of AI are also undeniable.

The challenge lies in navigating this new frontier responsibly and ethically. The gaming industry must find a way to harness the power of AI to enhance creativity, efficiency, and innovation, without sacrificing artistic integrity, displacing human talent, or compromising the quality of game experiences. The Black Ops 6 disclosure is just the beginning of this conversation, and the coming years will be crucial in shaping the role of AI in the future of gaming. The industry's response to player and developer concerns, and its commitment to ethical AI implementation, will ultimately determine whether AI becomes a force for good or a source of detriment in the world of game development. The future of gaming, in part, hinges on striking this delicate balance.
