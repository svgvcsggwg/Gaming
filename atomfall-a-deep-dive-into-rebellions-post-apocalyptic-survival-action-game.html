Atomfall: A Deep Dive into Rebellion's Post-Apocalyptic Survival Action Game

By Jessica
March 3, 2025

Rebellion Developments, the studio behind the popular Sniper Elite series, is venturing into new territory with their upcoming title, Atomfall. This survival-action game plunges players into a fictionalized quarantine zone in Northern England, five years after the real-life 1957 Windscale nuclear disaster. Unlike Rebellion's previous titles, Atomfall promises a unique blend of exploration, investigation, crafting, and intense combat, all wrapped in a distinctly British setting with a touch of science fiction, folk horror, and Cold War influences.

Atomfall is slated for release on March 27, 2025, and will be available on PC, PS4, PS5, Xbox One, and Xbox Series X/S. It will also be available on Game Pass day one.

Gameplay: A World of Exploration and Investigation

Atomfall is not your typical post-apocalyptic shooter. While combat is certainly a part of the experience, the game places a heavy emphasis on player-driven investigation and exploration. Rebellion has deliberately moved away from a traditional quest system, opting instead for a more organic approach where players must actively seek out leads and piece together the truth behind the Windscale disaster and the current state of the quarantine zone.

Art director Ryan Greene aptly described the game's core concept: "We like to think of it as the X-Files in the Cold War Lake District." This comparison highlights the game's focus on uncovering mysteries and dealing with the strange and unusual. Players won't be following objective markers; instead, they'll need to rely on their instincts, conversations with NPCs, and careful examination of the environment to progress.

The game world is presented from first person perspective, emphasizing exploration and survival.

Scavenging and Crafting: The Keys to Survival

Survival in Atomfall's quarantine zone is a constant struggle. Players will need to scavenge for resources, crafting materials, and even clues. Ruined buildings, abandoned bunkers, and the open countryside all hold potential treasures, but also dangers. Crafting plays a vital role, allowing players to create weapons, tools, and other essential items to aid in their survival.

The recent gameplay trailer, narrated by head of design Ben Fisher, emphasized the importance of careful searching and scavenging. Players will need to be mindful of their surroundings, not only to find valuable resources but also to avoid threats like roaming bandits and other dangers lurking within the zone. Craftables will include weapons like cricket bats, small bombs, and Molotov cocktails. However, players need to discover the necessary recipes throughout their journey.

Combat: Adaptability is Crucial

Combat in Atomfall is described as "desperate" and "frenetic." Ammunition is scarce, forcing players to make every shot count. The game seamlessly blends ranged combat with melee options, requiring players to adapt to changing situations. A unique mechanic involves managing the protagonist's heart rate. Strenuous activity, such as sprinting or engaging in combat, elevates the heart rate, leading to visual and auditory impairments. This adds a strategic layer to encounters, forcing players to consider their physical state when approaching a fight.

Stealth is also a viable option. Players can choose a more passive approach, utilizing stealth takedowns and avoiding direct confrontation whenever possible. In fact, it's been stated that the game can be completed without lethal force, offering players a choice in how they interact with the world and its inhabitants.

A Unique Setting: The Green and Unpleasant Land

Atomfall's setting is a stark contrast to the typical drab, grey wastelands often seen in post-apocalyptic games. Inspired by the British countryside, the quarantine zone features rolling green hills, lush valleys, and rural villages. However, this picturesque beauty hides a dark underbelly. Players will encounter cult-controlled ruins, natural caves, nuclear bunkers, and other unsettling locations as they explore the world.

Rebellion has drawn inspiration from various sources, including science fiction, folk horror, and the Cold War, to create a world that feels both familiar and alien. The game reimagines the Windscale disaster, weaving a fictional narrative around a real-world event, adding a layer of intrigue and grounding the fantastical elements in a tangible historical context.

A World Shaped by Player Choice

Atomfall offers players a remarkable degree of freedom. Without a traditional quest system, players are free to pursue any line of investigation they choose, right from the start. However, this freedom comes with consequences. Early choices, especially when poorly equipped, can lead to negative outcomes. The game encourages players to explore, talk to characters, and gather information, but it also warns that not all information is reliable, and some choices may close off other paths later on.

The interconnected nature of the game's stories means that pursuing one lead might impact another. This non-linear approach promises a high level of replayability, as players can experience the story differently with each playthrough. Hidden events can also drastically alter the game world. Specifically, events triggered (or missed) by the player can have significant influence on the enemy population.

Development: Iteration and Experimentation

The development of Atomfall has been a journey of iteration and experimentation. Lead designer Ben Fisher has spoken openly about the challenges of bringing such a unique vision to life. The team had to work in layers, constantly refining their ideas and adapting to the unexpected interactions between different game systems.

"We had to work in layers instead and kind of iterate the game over time," Fisher explained. He described the process as "stressful," highlighting the difficulty of translating paper plans into a functional and engaging game. This iterative approach, however, allowed the team to explore creative possibilities and push boundaries in ways they wouldn't have been able to with established franchises.

Atomfall is described as one of Rebellion's most experimental ventures. The studio embraced the opportunity to try new things, even when the outcome was uncertain. Fisher stated, "We took the creative pillars we were aiming for and just ran with them, so there's some experimental stuff you wouldn't try somewhere else because you can't guarantee a result."

The inspirations of *Atomfall* are *Fallout: New Vegas*, *Metro*, and *BioShock*.

Factions and Characters

The quarantine zone is populated by a variety of eccentric characters and cryptic organizations. These individuals and groups have been isolated from the outside world for years, developing their own unique beliefs and agendas. Player interactions with these factions are crucial, shaping the narrative and potentially leading to alliances or conflicts. It will be interesting to discern who is trustworthy and who is a foe.

These factions are more dynamic and are said to have differing beliefs. As an example, more peaceful factions will refuse to trade weapons.

Conclusion: A Promising and Unique Survival Experience

Atomfall is shaping up to be a unique and compelling addition to the survival-action genre. Its emphasis on player-driven investigation, challenging combat, and a distinctly British setting sets it apart from its contemporaries. Rebellion's willingness to experiment and iterate promises a game that is both intriguing and potentially groundbreaking. With its release just around the corner, players won't have to wait long to explore the mysteries and dangers of Atomfall's quarantine zone.
