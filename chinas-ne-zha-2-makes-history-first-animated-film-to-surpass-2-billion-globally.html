China's 'Ne Zha 2' Makes History: First Animated Film to Surpass $2 Billion Globally

By Ashley
March 3, 2025

In a groundbreaking achievement, the Chinese animated film Ne Zha 2 has become the first animated movie in history to surpass $2 billion at the global box office. This milestone was reached in a mere 33 days after its release, solidifying its status as a global phenomenon.

Record-Breaking Performance

Ne Zha 2's success story began during the lucrative Chinese New Year period, where it shattered numerous box office records. It consistently delivered the biggest single-day collections of all time during its run, and its momentum shows no signs of slowing down. Directed by Jiaozi and produced on a reported budget of $80 million, the film has resonated strongly with audiences, earning a remarkable 99% audience score on Rotten Tomatoes.

Dominating the Global and Domestic Box Office

The vast majority of Ne Zha 2's earnings come from China, with over $1.96 billion of its $2 billion global haul originating from the Middle Kingdom alone. This makes it the highest-grossing film ever in a single country, surpassing the previous record held by Star Wars: The Force Awakens, which earned $936 million domestically about a decade ago. Ne Zha 2 is poised to overtake The Force Awakens globally in the coming days. It will also soon surpass Avengers: Infinity War, becoming one of the top five highest-grossing movies of all time.

Joining the Elite $2 Billion Club

Ne Zha 2 is only the seventh film in history to reach the $2 billion mark globally. Once it surpasses Infinity War and The Force Awakens, it will only trail Titanic ($2.2 billion), Avatar: The Way of Water ($2.3 billion), Avengers: Endgame ($2.7 billion), and Avatar ($2.9 billion) on the all-time box office chart. Additionally, Ne Zha 2 is now the highest-grossing animated film ever, surpassing the $1.7 billion record set by Disney's Inside Out 2 just last year. Remarkably, Ne Zha 2 achieved this with a budget less than half that of Inside Out 2, which reportedly cost $200 million.

The Rise of the Chinese Film Market

The success of Ne Zha 2 highlights the significant evolution of the Chinese film market over the past decade. What was once a market heavily reliant on Hollywood imports has become increasingly self-sustaining. China now produces its own blockbuster hits, with three of this year's top five highest-grossing films originating from the country. The dominance of Chinese films like Ne Zha 2 has impacted the performance of Hollywood releases in China, with movies like Captain America: Brave New World struggling to find a significant audience.

Limited Domestic Release

Even with a limited theatrical release, Ne Zha 2 has also brought in over $15 million domestically.
