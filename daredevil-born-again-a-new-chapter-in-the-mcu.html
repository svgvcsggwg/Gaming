Daredevil: Born Again - A New Chapter in the MCU

By Felix
March 1, 2025

The Man Without Fear returns to the small screen in Daredevil: Born Again, a highly anticipated Disney+ series set to premiere on March 4, 2025. This series marks a significant moment for Marvel fans, acting as both a revival and continuation of the critically acclaimed Netflix series, Daredevil (2015-2018). The show promises a darker, grittier take on the superhero genre, drawing inspiration from classic New York crime stories while maintaining deep connections to the previous series.

 

The Return of Familiar Faces and a New Creative Vision

Daredevil: Born Again sees the return of Charlie Cox as Matt Murdock/Daredevil, the blind lawyer with heightened senses who fights crime as a masked vigilante. Vincent D'Onofrio reprises his role as Wilson Fisk/Kingpin, Daredevil's formidable and ruthless adversary. The series also welcomes back other beloved characters from the Netflix era, including Deborah Ann Woll as Karen Page, Elden Henson as Franklin "Foggy" Nelson, and Jon Bernthal as Frank Castle/The Punisher. Wilson Bethel will return as Benjamin "Dex" Poindexter, now more fully embracing his comic book persona of Bullseye. Ayelet Zurer also returns as Vanessa Fisk.

New additions to the cast include Margarita Levieva as Heather Glenn, and other actors like, Zabryna Guevara, Nikki M. James, Genneya Walton, Arty Froushan, Clark Johnson, Michael Gandolfini and. Notably, Matthew Lillard joins the Season 2 cast, though his role remains under wraps.

Behind the scenes, the series underwent a significant creative overhaul. Initially developed by Matt Corman and Chris Ord, the show's direction shifted dramatically in late 2023. Dario Scardapane, known for his work on Netflix's The Punisher, took over as showrunner. The acclaimed directing duo of Justin Benson and Aaron Moorhead, who previously worked on Marvel Studios' Moon Knight and Loki Season 2, were brought in to direct a significant block of episodes, and will continue their work into Season 2.

A Two-Part Epic: Season Structure and Release Dates

The original plan for Daredevil: Born Again was an ambitious 18-episode season. However, following the creative restructuring, the series was divided into two seasons. Season 1 will consist of nine episodes, premiering on Disney+ on March 4, 2025, with the first two episodes dropping simultaneously. Subsequent episodes will be released weekly. Season 2, which began filming on February 28, 2025, and it is confirmed to have eight episodes. Season 2 is slated for a 2026 release, potentially continuing Marvel's intention for an annual Daredevil release schedule.

The episode structure for Season One is as follow:

  • Episodes 1 and 2: March 4, 2025
  • Episode 3: March 12, 2025
  • Episode 4: March 19, 2025
  • Episodes 5 and 6: March 26, 2025

The release dates for the final three episodes of the season have yet to be revealed.

Plot and Tone: A Darker, More Expansive Narrative

Daredevil: Born Again promises a darker and more expansive story than its Netflix predecessor. While the Netflix series was praised for its grounded and noir-ish tone, the new series aims to embrace a broader scope, drawing inspiration from classic 1990s New York crime dramas like The Sopranos and King of New York. Showrunner Dario Scardapane has emphasized that the new series will be "much darker" than the previous iteration.

The official plot synopsis highlights the central conflict between Matt Murdock and Wilson Fisk: "Matt Murdock (Charlie Cox), a blind lawyer with heightened abilities is fighting for justice through his bustling law firm, while former mob boss Wilson Fisk (Vincent D'Onofrio) pursues his own political endeavors in New York. When their past identities begin to emerge, both men find themselves on an inevitable collision course."

The series will pick up some time after the Netflix show, integrating Matt Murdock's experiences in other MCU projects (such as Spider-Man: No Way Home and She-Hulk) but not necessarily dwelling on every detail. The show will focus primarily on the core Daredevil story and the ongoing battle against crime in New York City. The original "Born Again" comic book storyline features Karen Page selling Matt Murdock's identity for drug money which culminates with Kingpin ruining Matt's life. Although elements of this arc have been hinted, it isn’t clear to what extent this plot will be present in the show.

The Bullseye Showdown: A Glimpse into the Action

A recently released clip from IGN provides a tantalizing glimpse into the brutal action sequences fans can expect. The clip showcases a visceral fight between Daredevil and Bullseye, reminiscent of the iconic hallway fights from the Netflix series. The scene unfolds in a stairwell and extends to a rooftop, highlighting the physicality and intensity of the combat. Bullseye, now fully embracing his villainous persona, attacks Daredevil with knives and improvised weapons. Daredevil, despite sustaining multiple stab wounds, remains relentless in his pursuit.

Cast and Characters: A Detailed Breakdown

Here's a closer look at the confirmed cast and their characters:

  • Charlie Cox as Matt Murdock/Daredevil: The blind lawyer who fights crime as a vigilante, possessing heightened senses and exceptional combat skills.
  • Vincent D'Onofrio as Wilson Fisk/Kingpin: A powerful and ruthless crime lord, and Daredevil's primary antagonist.
  • Deborah Ann Woll as Karen Page: A former secretary at Nelson & Murdock, and a close friend and confidante to Matt and Foggy.
  • Elden Henson as Franklin "Foggy" Nelson: Matt Murdock's best friend and law partner.
  • Jon Bernthal as Frank Castle/The Punisher: A vigilante who employs lethal methods to combat crime.
  • Wilson Bethel as Benjamin "Dex" Poindexter/Bullseye: A highly skilled marksman and assassin with a psychopathic personality.
  • Ayelet Zurer as Vanessa Marianna-Fisk: Wilson Fisk's wife.
  • Margarita Levieva as Heather Glenn: A new character, and a love interest for Matt Murdock.
  • Other Confirmed Actors:, Zabryna Guevara, Nikki M. James, Genneya Walton, Arty Froushan, Clark Johnson, Michael Gandolfini and.

Matthew Lillard has joined the cast for season two. The role that Lillard is going to play is being kept under wraps.

Creative Team: Showrunner and Directors

The creative minds behind *Daredevil: Born Again* bring a wealth of experience and a commitment to delivering a high-quality, action-packed, and character-driven series:

  • Dario Scardapane (Showrunner): Scardapane, known for his work on *The Punisher*, serves as the showrunner, guiding the overall narrative and creative direction. He emphasizes a focus on longer, character-driven scenes, aiming to balance intense action sequences with meaningful character development.
  • Justin Benson and Aaron Moorhead (Directors): This directing duo, acclaimed for their work on *Moon Knight* and *Loki* Season 2, are responsible for directing a significant portion of the series. They bring a unique visual style and a proven track record of crafting compelling stories within the MCU. They are confirmed to return for Season 2.
  • Philip J. Silvera (2nd Unit Director/Stunt Coordinator): Silvera's involvement ensures that the action sequences will be both thrilling and realistic, building upon the legacy of the Netflix series' acclaimed fight choreography.
  • The Newton Brothers (Composers): Taylor Newton and Andrew Grush create the musical score.

Addressing Criticisms and Looking to the Future

Dario Scardapane has openly acknowledged some of the criticisms leveled at the Netflix *Daredevil* series, particularly moments where the show focused heavily on characters "grousing about their lot in life." He has stated that *Born Again* will prioritize action and forward momentum, aiming for a faster pace and more dynamic storytelling. He specifically mentioned his desire to avoid lengthy scenes of characters simply talking about what it means to be a hero, preferring to show them *in action*.

Production and Filming

Filming for Daredevil: Born Again primarily takes place in New York City, capturing the gritty atmosphere of Hell's Kitchen. Filming for Season 2 is currently underway, having commenced on February 28, 2025, and scheduled to continue through August 2025.

A New Era for Daredevil

Daredevil: Born Again represents a new era for the character, integrating him firmly within the Marvel Cinematic Universe while retaining the core elements that made the Netflix series so successful. With a talented cast, a dedicated creative team, and a commitment to delivering a dark, action-packed, and character-driven story, the series is poised to captivate both longtime fans and new viewers alike. The confirmed return of key characters, the introduction of new faces, and the promise of intense action set the stage for an exciting new chapter in the saga of the Man Without Fear.
