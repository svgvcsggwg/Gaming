Galaxy A56 vs. Redmi Note 14 Pro Plus vs. Poco X7 Pro: Mid-Range Smartphone Showdown

By Felix
March 2, 2025

In the competitive world of smartphones, choosing the best device according to the specific needs of each user can be difficult. The Samsung Galaxy A56, Poco X7 Pro, and Redmi Note 14 Pro Plus are very close in terms of technical specifications, but each has its own unique features that can influence the final choice. In this article, we will examine the strengths and weaknesses of each by comparing all three models in detail to see which is the best mid-range phone for you.

Design and Build

Galaxy A56: With a beautiful design and good build quality, the Galaxy A56 uses Gorilla Glass Victus on the front and back, which increases its resistance to scratches and impacts. In addition, its aluminum frame provides a stylish and modern look in addition to increasing its strength. This phone weighs 197 grams, which is slightly heavier than its competitors.


Back and front panel and color scheme of the Galaxy A56

Poco X7 Pro: The body of the Poco X7 Pro is made of silicone polymer, which is typically less resistant to scratches than glass. The phone comes in two models with weights of 195 grams and 198 grams, but overall it is almost the same weight as the Galaxy A56 and lighter than the Redmi Note 14 Pro Plus. Also, the Poco phone has a wider variety of colors in terms of design, which may be more attractive to some people.

Front and back panel of the Xiaomi Poco X7 Pro

Redmi Note 14 Pro Plus: The front of the Redmi Note 14 Pro Plus is covered with Gorilla Glass Victus 2 and, at 210.8 grams, it is heavier than its competitors. This phone has a high build quality, but its greater weight can be a bit annoying for some users.


Xiaomi Redmi Note 14 Pro Plus Mobile

Display

The Galaxy A56, Poco X7 Pro, and Redmi Note 14 Pro Plus all feature AMOLED displays with a refresh rate of 120Hz, but there are differences in their quality and features.

Galaxy A56: The Galaxy A56 features a 6.7-inch Super AMOLED display with a resolution of 2340 x 1080 pixels. The display supports a maximum brightness of 1200 nits (in HBM mode) and the HDR10 Plus standard, which will perform very well in various lighting conditions. The display also supports Always-on Display, which is a popular and useful feature for many users.


Samsung Galaxy A56 Front and Back Colors

Poco X7 Pro: The Poco X7 Pro has a 6.67-inch display with a resolution of 2712 x 1220 pixels and excellent color accuracy. The display supports HDR10 Plus and Dolby Vision, and offers a maximum brightness of 3200 nits along with 12-bit color depth, which will be a great experience for watching HDR content. The device also has a touch sampling rate of 480 Hz, which provides a high response speed for games and everyday applications.


Front and back panel of the Xiaomi Poco X7 Pro mobile phone

Redmi Note 14 Pro Plus: Like the Poco X7 Pro, the Redmi Note 14 Pro Plus uses a 6.67-inch AMOLED display with a resolution of 2712 x 1220 pixels and supports HDR10 Plus and Dolby Vision standards. This model of the Xiaomi phone has a maximum brightness of 300 nits, which is impressive compared to other phones and makes it easy to watch content in very bright environments.

Overall, the Poco X7 Pro and Redmi Note 14 Pro+ have better displays, at least on paper, thanks to higher brightness, better color accuracy, and support for Dolby Vision and HDR10 Plus. The Galaxy A56 falls behind its competitors in this area, given its higher price.

Performance and Chipset

Galaxy A56: The Galaxy A56 uses the Exynos 1580 chip with a 4nm manufacturing process, which includes 8 processing cores with a maximum frequency of 2.91 GHz. This chip, together with the Xclipse 540 GPU, can provide good performance for everyday tasks, gaming, and multitasking.

Poco X7 Pro: The Poco X7 Pro uses the Dimensity 8400 Ultra chip, which has 8 cores at 3.25 GHz and uses the Mali-G720 MC7 GPU. This chip is specifically designed for better gaming performance and can provide a smooth and lag-free experience in heavy games and applications.

Redmi Note 14 Pro Plus: Redmi Note 14 Pro Plus uses the 3rd generation Snapdragon 7s chip with 8 processing cores. The main cores of this chip operate at a frequency of 2.5 GHz and also use the Adreno 710 graphics processor, which has very good performance. The device seems to be an attractive option especially for mobile gamers.

Cameras

The cameras on the Poco X7 Pro and Redmi Note 14 Pro Plus each have their own unique features that appeal to users with different needs.

The Galaxy A56’s 50-megapixel main camera features autofocus, optical image stabilization (OIS), and a 1/1.56-inch sensor, so it delivers good image quality even in low-light conditions. The 12MP f/2.2 ultrawide camera with a 123-degree field of view and the 5MP f/2.4 macro camera, along with 4K video recording, make this a good choice for everyday photography. However, compared to other competitors, the ultrawide and macro camera capabilities of this device are lacking.
