Shrek is Back! First Trailer for 'Shrek 5' Unleashes a New Chapter with Zendaya as Shrek's Daughter

By Felix
March 1, 2025

 

The swamp is calling once again! Get ready to return to the beloved fairytale world as Universal and DreamWorks have officially dropped the first trailer for the highly anticipated Shrek 5. In a magical announcement that has sent ripples of excitement across the internet, the trailer not only confirms the return of our favorite ogre and his hilarious companions but also introduces a fresh face to the franchise: the incredibly talented Zendaya, who will be lending her voice to Shrek's daughter.

For fans who have been yearning for a return to Far Far Away, this news is nothing short of a dream come true. The trailer promises a delightful blend of the classic Shrek humor and heart, with a touch of modern flair thanks to the exciting addition to the voice cast and a glimpse at a potentially updated animation style.

The Gang's All Here (and There's a New Ogre in Town!)

The core of Shrek's charm has always been its iconic voice cast, and thankfully, Shrek 5 is bringing back the dream team. Prepare to be transported back to the swamp with the unmistakable voices of:

  • Mike Myers as Shrek: The lovable, grumpy ogre with a heart of gold (and a penchant for swamp-dwelling) is back in all his glory. Myers' comedic timing and distinctive Scottish accent are integral to Shrek's character, and his return is essential for capturing the nostalgic magic of the franchise.
  • Eddie Murphy as Donkey: The fast-talking, endlessly optimistic Donkey, Shrek's loyal and hilarious sidekick, is confirmed to be back, voiced once again by the incomparable Eddie Murphy. Murphy's energetic and improvisational style brings Donkey to life, making him an audience favorite.
  • Cameron Diaz as Princess Fiona: The beautiful and fiercely independent Princess Fiona, who is more than capable of rescuing herself (and delivering a mean ogre roar), will once again be voiced by Cameron Diaz. Diaz's portrayal of Fiona as both regal and down-to-earth is a cornerstone of the Shrek saga.

But the exciting news doesn't stop with the returning trio. Shrek 5 is set to introduce a new generation to the swamp with Zendaya joining the cast as Felicia, Shrek and Fiona's daughter. This casting choice is a stroke of genius, injecting fresh star power into the franchise while also promising a compelling new character dynamic. Zendaya, known for her roles in blockbuster hits like Spider-Man and Dune, brings a significant fanbase and undeniable talent to the Shrek universe. Her voice acting debut in this role is highly anticipated, and fans are eager to see how she brings Felicia to life.

The trailer also confirmed the return of another beloved character: Pinocchio. While specific details about Pinocchio's role in Shrek 5 are still under wraps, his inclusion suggests that the film will continue to weave in familiar fairytale characters and storylines in its signature irreverent style.

However, there's also a notable absence. While many hoped for the return of the swashbuckling feline, Puss in Boots, voiced by Antonio Banderas, the trailer and initial reports suggest that he may not be part of this particular Shrek adventure. This is somewhat surprising, considering Puss in Boots' popularity and the success of his spin-off films. It remains to be seen if Puss in Boots will make a surprise appearance or if his absence is a deliberate choice to focus on other characters and storylines.

Behind the Camera: A New Director and Familiar Writers

Steering the helm of Shrek 5 is Walt Dohrn, who previously directed the Trolls trilogy for DreamWorks. Dohrn's experience with vibrant animation and comedic storytelling makes him a fitting choice to take on the Shrek universe. He is known for his work on Trolls (2016), Trolls World Tour (2020), and Trolls Band Together (2023), all of which were commercially successful and praised for their visual style and musical elements.

The screenplay for Shrek 5 is penned by Michael McCullers, known for his work on comedies like Austin Powers: The Spy Who Shagged Me and Baby Mama. McCullers' background in comedy writing suggests that Shrek 5 will retain the franchise's signature humor and witty dialogue. He is collaborating on the story with Chris Meldandari, the founder and CEO of Illumination, the animation studio behind the Despicable Me and Minions franchises. Meldandari's involvement, while primarily known for Illumination's work, could bring a fresh perspective to the Shrek universe, potentially influencing the comedic style and character dynamics.

Assisting Dohrn in bringing the animation to life is Brad Abelson, who will serve as co-director. Abelson's role suggests a strong focus on the animation itself, and his expertise will be crucial in delivering the visual spectacle that audiences expect from a Shrek film.

A Legacy of Laughter: Looking Back at the Shrek Phenomenon

The Shrek franchise has undeniably left an indelible mark on animation and popular culture. Beginning with the original Shrek in 2001, the series quickly became a global phenomenon, subverting fairytale tropes and delivering humor that resonated with both children and adults.

  • Shrek (2001): The film that started it all, Shrek, was a groundbreaking achievement in animation. Its irreverent humor, heartwarming story, and memorable characters propelled it to box office success, grossing $487 million worldwide. More importantly, Shrek made history by becoming the first-ever winner of the Academy Award for Best Animated Feature, solidifying its place in cinematic history and paving the way for future animated films to be recognized for their artistic merit.
  • Shrek 2 (2004): Riding high on the success of the first film, Shrek 2 surpassed all expectations, becoming an even bigger box office hit. It earned a staggering $935.5 million globally, making it one of the highest-grossing animated films of all time. Shrek 2 is often considered by many fans to be the pinnacle of the franchise, expanding on the characters and world while delivering even more laughs and emotional depth.
  • Shrek the Third (2007) and Shrek Forever After (2010): While these sequels didn't quite reach the same critical and commercial heights as the first two films, they were still significant box office successes. Shrek the Third grossed $813.4 million worldwide, and Shrek Forever After earned $756.2 million. These films further explored the characters' lives and relationships, albeit with a slightly less enthusiastic reception compared to their predecessors.

Beyond the main Shrek films, the franchise expanded with spin-offs centered around the charismatic Puss in Boots.

  • Puss in Boots (2011): The first spin-off film, Puss in Boots, showcased the character's solo adventures and proved to be a hit, grossing $555 million worldwide. It demonstrated the enduring appeal of the Shrek universe and the potential for further expansion.
  • Puss in Boots: The Last Wish (2022): Over a decade later, Puss in Boots returned in Puss in Boots: The Last Wish, which was met with critical acclaim and box office success. It grossed over $485.3 million globally and was lauded for its stunning animation style, emotional depth, and engaging story. The Last Wish revitalized the franchise and proved that there was still a strong appetite for stories set in the Shrek universe.

The combined global box office gross of the Shrek franchise and its spin-offs is well over $4 billion, making it one of the most successful animated franchises in history. This financial success is a testament to the franchise's enduring popularity and its ability to connect with audiences across generations.

More Than Just Box Office Numbers: The Cultural Impact of Shrek

The Shrek franchise's impact extends far beyond its impressive box office figures. It has permeated popular culture in numerous ways:

  • Subversion of Fairytale Tropes: Shrek famously flipped traditional fairytale narratives on their head. Instead of a handsome prince rescuing a damsel in distress, we have an ogre and a princess who embraces her ogre form. This deconstruction of classic tropes resonated with audiences and paved the way for more self-aware and humorous animated storytelling.
  • Humor for All Ages: Shrek's humor is cleverly layered, appealing to both children and adults. Kids enjoy the slapstick and visual gags, while adults appreciate the witty dialogue, pop culture references, and satirical elements. This broad appeal is a key factor in the franchise's enduring success.
  • Memorable Characters: From Shrek and Donkey to Fiona and Puss in Boots, the Shrek franchise boasts a cast of unforgettable characters. Each character is distinct and well-developed, contributing to the rich tapestry of the Shrek universe. These characters have become iconic and instantly recognizable.
  • Animation Innovation: The original Shrek was praised for its groundbreaking CGI animation at the time of its release. The franchise has continued to push animation boundaries, with Puss in Boots: The Last Wish being particularly lauded for its unique and visually stunning animation style, blending CGI with a painterly, storybook aesthetic. The trailer for Shrek 5 also hints at a potentially evolved animation style, sparking curiosity about the visual direction of the new film.
  • Internet Meme Culture: Shrek has become a significant part of internet meme culture. Characters, scenes, and lines from the films are frequently used in memes, demonstrating the franchise's continued relevance and cultural penetration. "Shrek is love, Shrek is life" is just one example of the many memes that have sprung from the Shrek universe, further cementing its place in online culture.

What to Expect from Shrek 5? Speculations and Anticipation

While the trailer is just a teaser, and concrete plot details are still scarce, we can speculate on what Shrek 5 might bring based on the franchise's history and the information available:

  • Family Focus: The introduction of Shrek and Fiona's daughter, Felicia, voiced by Zendaya, strongly suggests that family dynamics will be a central theme in Shrek 5. We can expect to see Shrek and Fiona navigating the challenges of parenthood, perhaps with Felicia embarking on her own adventure or facing some kind of ogre-related coming-of-age story. Exploring the parent-child relationship within the Shrek universe offers fresh storytelling possibilities.
  • New Animation Style: Several reports mention a "changed animation" style hinted at in the trailer. This could mean a further evolution of the visual style seen in Puss in Boots: The Last Wish, perhaps incorporating even more stylized and painterly elements. A visual refresh could be a way to keep the franchise feeling modern and exciting for audiences.
  • Classic Shrek Humor: Fans will undoubtedly expect the signature Shrek humor to be present in full force. This includes fairytale parodies, pop culture references, witty banter, and slapstick comedy. The involvement of Michael McCullers as screenwriter, known for his comedic work, suggests that the humor will be a priority.
  • Heartfelt Story: Beneath the humor and fairytale subversion, the Shrek films have always had a heart. Themes of acceptance, self-love, and the importance of friendship and family have been woven throughout the series. Shrek 5 is likely to continue this tradition, balancing the laughs with genuine emotional moments.
  • Potential for New Characters and Worlds: While the return of familiar characters like Pinocchio is confirmed, Shrek 5 could also introduce new fairytale figures and explore previously unseen corners of Far Far Away. The Shrek universe is vast and rich with possibilities, and a new film offers the chance to expand it further.
  • Release Date: Currently, Shrek 5 is slated for a theatrical release on December 25, 2026. This Christmas release date positions it as a family-friendly holiday blockbuster, aiming to capture a broad audience during the festive season.

Audience Buzz and Anticipation

The announcement of Shrek 5 and the trailer release have generated significant buzz online. Social media platforms are abuzz with excitement, with fans expressing their anticipation for the film. The casting of Zendaya has been particularly well-received, adding to the film's appeal and drawing in new audiences.

  • Nostalgia Factor: For many, Shrek is a beloved part of their childhood. The return of the franchise taps into this nostalgia, with fans eager to revisit the characters and world they grew up with. The original Shrek films hold a special place in the hearts of millennials and Gen Z, and Shrek 5 has the potential to reignite that passion.
  • Family Entertainment: Shrek has always been a franchise that families can enjoy together. Shrek 5 is expected to continue this tradition, offering entertainment that appeals to both children and adults, making it a highly anticipated family movie event.
  • Zendaya Effect: Zendaya's involvement is a major draw for a younger demographic. Her popularity and influence are undeniable, and her casting is likely to bring in fans who may not have been as familiar with the Shrek franchise previously. This strategic casting move broadens the film's potential audience and ensures its relevance in the current entertainment landscape.
  • Animation Renaissance: Animated films are currently experiencing a renaissance, with both critical and commercial successes in recent years. Puss in Boots: The Last Wish, Spider-Man: Across the Spider-Verse, and Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles: Mutant Mayhem have all demonstrated the power and artistry of modern animation. Shrek 5 arrives at a time when audiences are eager for high-quality animated storytelling, and expectations are high for the film to deliver visually and narratively.

Final Thoughts: Once Upon a Time in 2026...

Shrek 5 is poised to be a major cinematic event in 2026. The return of the core voice cast, the exciting addition of Zendaya, and the promise of a new chapter in the Shrek saga have all combined to create a significant wave of anticipation. Whether you're a longtime fan of the ogre from the swamp or a newcomer intrigued by the buzz, Shrek 5 is shaping up to be a must-see film. Get ready to journey back to Far Far Away, because adventure, laughter, and maybe even a little bit of ogre magic are on their way. December 2026 can't come soon enough!
